## Engagement Weight Multipliers

The X algorithm uses a weighted scoring formula. Understanding these weights explains why some tactics matter far more than others:

| Engagement Type | Weight vs. Like |
|---|---|
| Reply chain with original author | **150x** |
| Retweet / Repost | **20x** |
| Profile click | **12x** |
| Link click | **11x** |
| Bookmark | **10x** |
| Like | **1x (baseline)** |

**Implication:** A post that gets 5 replies is algorithmically more valuable than one that gets 750 likes. Design content to generate conversation, not applause.

**Implication:** Bookmarks signal "I want to save this for later" — create reference-worthy content. Lists, frameworks, data breakdowns, and predictions get bookmarked.

## Post Half-Life

A post's effective engagement window is approximately **18 minutes**. After one hour, it's essentially gone algorithmically unless being actively retweeted. This means:

- First-hour engagement is critical — activate seed supporters immediately after posting
- Post timing matters enormously (see below)
- Replies received in the first 18 minutes are worth dramatically more than later ones

## Optimal Posting Windows (Crypto/Solana Audience)

**Primary:** Tuesday–Thursday, 8–11 AM UTC
- Catches US East Coast morning (3–6 AM ET, builders who check before work)
- Catches EU mid-morning (9 AM–noon CET)
- Peak Solana ecosystem activity overlaps this window

**Secondary:** 20:00–23:00 UTC
- Captures Asia-Pacific and late EU evening
- Good for second post of the day

**Avoid:** Friday evening through Sunday for anything requiring maximum reach.

**Posting frequency for tech/crypto accounts:** 19 posts/week (3–4/day) is the data-backed peak. But quality always beats volume — 5 great posts outperform 20 noise posts.

## What the Algorithm Penalizes

- **Links in the first tweet of a thread** — A/B testing (Buffer, 2025) found removing a link from an identical tweet produced a **1,700% reach increase**. This is not a preference — it is the single highest-leverage formatting decision available. Never put a link in tweet 1. Place links in the first reply or in tweet 2+ of a thread.
- **Low-engagement rate per follower** — a small account with high engagement gets more distribution than a large account with low engagement
- **Duplicate/repetitive content** — posting the same update multiple times tanks reach

## Thread Best Practices

- Spend 30–40% of writing time on the hook (first tweet) — it determines if the thread is read
- One idea per tweet — atomic and digestible
- Number tweets: 1/, 2/, 3/ format aids readability
- 4–8 tweets optimal — longer threads lose readers
- Last tweet: clear call to action (reply, repost, bookmark)
- **Never** put a link in tweet 1

### Hook Formulas That Work for Technical Crypto Projects

1. **Bold + specific claim**: "90% of [audience] are making a mistake that costs them [X]. Here's how to fix it."
2. **Provocative question**: "Is [consensus belief] actually true? The data says something very different."
3. **Contrarian take**: "Everyone is wrong about [topic]. Here's the actual data."
4. **Numbered format**: "5 things [audience] needs to know about [domain] right now"
5. **The reveal**: "We just [accomplishment]. Here's what we learned."

## Reply Strategy Data

- **50 quality replies per day** is cited as the growth threshold for consistent follower acquisition
- Reply within the **first 6 hours** while the conversation is still active
- Target accounts with **10x–100x** your follower count — exposes you to their audience
- When your reply gains traction, it boosts the entire thread (halo effect)
- Substantive replies (new information, genuine question, specific position) generate followers; "great point!" replies generate nothing
